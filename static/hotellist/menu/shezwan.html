Shezwan Chutney

Ingredients

  • 15-18 Dry Red Kashmiri Chillis*
  • 3-4 tablespoons Sesame Oil or any Cooking Oil
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped Onion or 3-4 Shallots
  • 10-12 small Garlic Cloves, chopped (approx. 2½ tablespoons)
  • 1/2 tablespoon finely chopped Ginger (approx. 1-inch)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Dark Soy Sauce or 1/2 teaspoon Light Soy Sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon Black Pepper Powder
  • 1½ tablespoon Tomato Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Sugar
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  1. Remove stems from dry red chillis. Cut chilli into halves, open them and remove seeds. Discard the seeds.
  2. Soak chilli halves in warm water for 30 minutes.
  3. Drain excess water from soaked red chillis. Transfer them to a small jar of a grinder. Add 2 tablespoons water.
  4. Grind into a smooth paste.
  5. Heat 3-4 tablespoons oil in a pan over medium flame. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns light pink.
  6. Add finely chopped ginger and garlic, sauté for around a minute until raw smell of garlic goes off.
  7. Add ground red chilli paste. Mix well and sauté for a minute.
  8. Add 1½ tablespoon tomato sauce.
  9. Add 1 tablespoon vinegar.
  10. Add 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder, sugar and salt.
  11. Mix well and cook for a minute. Add 1/4 cup water and mix well.
